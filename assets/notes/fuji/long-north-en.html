Mt. Fuji · North route

Asagiri Plateau

Open fields and a clean horizon make the “person + Fuji” ratio easy to control. Minimal yet not empty; windy days feel cinematic.

Saiko lakeside

Water softens the light for a gentler look. Great for quiet emotions: walking, hugging, glancing back at the mountain.

Nagasaki Park

Many angles in one area. Easy to switch compositions: subject foreground, lakeshore mid-ground, Fuji in the background.

Kawaguchiko Lawson

A signature “Fuji + street” landmark. Keep composition and tones restrained to avoid a tourist look; elevate with pose and details.

Hikawa Clock Shop

Deep street perspective gives strong leading lines and a surprise Fuji reveal. Great for city-story moments: walking, waiting at lights, looking back.

Mt. Fuji Station

Lots of elements to create “departure/arrival” feelings. Perfect for everyday interactions: drinks in hand, fixing hair/clothes, pre-ride moments.

Lake Yamanaka (Hiranohama)

Lake and sky create a relaxed, romantic mood. With good light, the background layers are very clean and breathable.
